Ethical decision

The situation:
A friend who has a severe eating disorder was in a really bad way physically (hadn't eaten to the point where they were struggling to walk around). They assured me they would be ok, and that they would eat soon, and that tehy were not at their worst, and that they were "fine". I had to decide whether I would call emergency services, which in the circumstance would lead to them being comitted to an intitution against their will, for an unknown amount of time. They had already said that they never want to go back to this place, and the situation would completely derail their life (what life they had left). I was also aware that I was one of the only people in their life that they actually trusted, and sending them to hospital would mean they woud lose that. I was fearful they might die.

Role of values in my decision
I had to weigh up compassion I had for her mental situation, vs her physical well being. I was open to the idea that I didn't fully understand her situation, both mentally and physically, and I had to use my best judgement to decide someone elses future for them. I ended up not calling emergency services.

Reflecting on the decision
I am still uncomfortable with the decision, as I think I allowed my best judgement to be swayed by my compassion for their situation. However, I'm also aware of how important autonomy is to my friend, and the alternative would have removed all autonomy from their life. I don't have the benefit of hindsight on this yet as it's an ongoing situation, and as of writing this I might still call emergency services today. I actually thought writing this might help me process the information and make a decision about how to move forward, but I'm still very much in the same position.

Conflict resolution example

I was the coach of a hockey team going to an under 21 national tournament. Some of the guys in the team would at times show up hung-over to trainings, which would affect their performance and the ability of others to get the most out of the training. This was causing tension in the team. So as a group we developed a set of team "ethics" which each of the guys agreed to.

The strategy was very effective, I think mostly because it created a sense of mutual buy in from the group. The biggest change I would make would be to do a similar process right from the start of the season!
