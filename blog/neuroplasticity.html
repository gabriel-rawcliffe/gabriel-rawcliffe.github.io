Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity refers to the brains ability to strengthn existing and form new neural pathways and networks throughout life. When we learn something new, or build on an existing skill, we are tapping into the brains ability to change and develop.

By understanding neuroplasicity and the mechanisms that build and strengthen neural pathways we can focus our learning into methods that will give us the best chances of success. Also simply knowing that skills and knowledge are things that our brain is designed to develop over time actually makes us more likely to succeed in building new pathways in our brain

Methods to Improve Neuroplasticity

  • Learn new things: Challenging your brain with new stimuli (e.g. learning a new skill, instument or hobby) stimulates the formation of new neural connections.
  • Physical exercise Regular exercise can enhance neuroplasticity and cognitive function by increasing levels of the protein, brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which promotes the growth and survival of brain cells.
  • Mental stimulation: Doing puzzles, brain-teasers, reading, or playing strategy games improves.
  • Socialise: Interacting with others, conversation, and group activies promote neuplasticity.
  • Mindfulness: Practicing mindfulness and meditiation improve focus, reduce stress and enhance brain health, which in-turn promotes neuroplasicity.
  • Sleep: Getting enough sleep is important for memory consolidation
  • Healthy diet: Nutrition is important for brain health; get enough omega-3, antioxidants, and vitamin-B.
  • Practice: Repetition of a skill either physically, or mentally, strengthens existing neural pathways. Diverse practice methods will give better results.

Growth Mindset

In general a Growth Mindset is about our perception of learning obstacles and set-backs. If we have a closed mindset when we encounter something we don't understand or get something wrong, we would quicklyy close ourselves off to learning opportunities. Closed mindset in the context of learning is typically very outcome focussed, where the goal is to get the correct answer.
If we have a growth mindset, when we don't understand something, or get an answer wrong, it is perceived as an opportunity to develop our skills and knowledge. Growth mindset is typically more process oriented, where we perceive success when we have actively engaged with the process of learning, even if we haven't yet got to a "correct answer".

This is important to my learning journey as I think I can sometimes to too focussed on finding an "answer", particularly if I feel others around me know how to do something. However, this is such a short-sighted approach, as unless I am using the answer to properly engage with the process that was taken to get there, it will not help me in future challenges.

Integration into my learning plan

Based on the information about neuroplasticity, I will be taking time within my learning plan to keep my brain primed to learn! This will include time for exercise, socialising, good food, games, and rest.

I will also make sure that whenever I am learning new things, or encounter a problem, I see it as an opportunity to grow. I will focus on the process of learning, not the goal of getting a specific answer.
