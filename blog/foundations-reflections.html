Reflections on "Foundations"

Things I learnt about myself:

  • Too worried about knowing the "answer": I realised that early in the process I was concerned about getting the "right answer" instead of coming to a better understandng about what was going on.
  • I'm self conscious about my work: I think that because I have been an expert in my field for so long, being a complete beginner makes me feel very vulnerable, and so the idea of people seeing my work makes me really nervous
  • I feel a bit weird about people consciously listening to me: I think because I feel like I'm forcing someone to listen to my rambling. I think it would be different if I felt like I was conveying an interesting idea.

Role of values, empathy, and slef awareness in learning and programming

Our values tend to effect how we approach every situation, problem, or conflict, so if we are self-aware about what our values are, and where they come from it makes us better able to recocgnise why we might feel a particular way, or how we might react to a given situation. This also allows us to be more empathetic to other peoples positions as we don't all have the same values. In learning and coding these are all important, as in either environment we will likely encounter problems we need to overcome, or situations where we need to be empathetic to someone elses position, previous experiences, or current knowledge. I think being self-aware about our own limitations and strengths is extremely important for learning.
